It's great to see that you are motivated to compete in the 1002th edition of the Helvetic Coding Contest (hc2)! I am Heidi, the coding cow and mascot of this Swiss-wide event! At hc2, you will live a vibrant atmosphere of solving funny algorithmic challenges, staggered over different levels of difficulty, and socializing with contestants, staff, organizers and industrials from all over the country! Myself, I will hit the road on that day and trust once more your impressive coding skills to take me through breathtaking adventures and safely back to my pasturage afterwards.

Having travelled through Switzerland in 2010, all around the world in 2011, through the time-space continuum in 2012, I am ready to enter the cyberspace this year! My friends, the marmots, provided me with the key to enter the cyberspace and travel through it.

Cyberspace is huge, and, knowing me, it will be a cakewalk to get lost in there! That’s why I’ll travel around by using just this single key, which works as follows:

Each position in cyberspace is represented by a terribly long sequence of lowercase characters ‘a’ to ‘z’.

Applying the key brings me to a next position, which is found by substituting all characters as requested by the key:

abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz
??????????????????????????

Provided that the description of my starting location contains all 26 characters at least once, can you tell me how many different locations I can visit with that key?

Illustration

To make sure you understand the task correctly, consider this reduced example of a cybersubspace. Let’s say the considered cybersubspace is spun by only the first 6 letters of the alphabet and the key is bacefd:

abcdef
bacefd

Letting Heidi start at the lower-left corner, which happens to be cdeadbeef, her journey would be:

cdeadbeef -> cefbeaffd -> cfdafbdde -> cdebdaeef -> cefaebffd -> cfdbfadde -> cdeadbeef

allowing her to visit 6 distinct locations.
